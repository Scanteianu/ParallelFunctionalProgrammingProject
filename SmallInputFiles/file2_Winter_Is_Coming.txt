<p>"<b>Winter Is Coming</b>" is the <a href="/wiki/Series_premiere" class="mw-redirect" title="Series premiere">series premiere</a> episode of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> <a href="/wiki/Medieval_fantasy" class="mw-redirect" title="Medieval fantasy">medieval fantasy</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i>. The first episode of the <a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones_(season_1)" title="Game of Thrones (season 1)">first season</a>, it was written by the show creators <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> and <a href="/wiki/D._B._Weiss" title="D. B. Weiss">D. B. Weiss</a>, in a faithful adaptation of the first chapters of <a href="/wiki/George_R._R._Martin" title="George R. R. Martin">George R. R. Martin</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/A_Game_of_Thrones" title="A Game of Thrones">A Game of Thrones</a></i>. The episode was directed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Van_Patten" title="Tim Van Patten">Tim Van Patten</a>, redoing the work done by director <a href="/wiki/Tom_McCarthy_(director)" title="Tom McCarthy (director)">Tom McCarthy</a> in an unaired pilot.
</p><p>As the first episode of the series, it introduces the <a href="/wiki/World_of_A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="World of A Song of Ice and Fire">setting</a> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Game_of_Thrones_characters" title="List of Game of Thrones characters">main characters</a> of the show. The episode centers on the Stark family, and how its lord, <a href="/wiki/Ned_Stark" title="Ned Stark">Eddard "Ned" Stark</a>, gets involved in the court politics after the king chooses Eddard to replace his recently deceased chief administrator ("Hand of the King"). The episode received largely positive reviews, and was seen initially by 2.2 million viewers. A week before the episode first aired, HBO made the first 15 minutes available as an Internet preview.
</p><p>The title of the episode is the motto (referred to as "House Words" in-universe) of <a href="/wiki/House_Stark" class="mw-redirect" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>, which is spoken several times in the episode and in the series.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beyond_the_Wall">Beyond the Wall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Beyond the Wall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the continent of <a href="/wiki/Westeros" class="mw-redirect" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>, rangers of the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" class="mw-redirect" title="Night&#39;s Watch">Night's Watch</a> scout wildling movements beyond the Wall, the massive ice barrier to the north, and discover demonic White Walkers and wildlings turned to undead wights. Will, the sole surviving ranger, flees South.
</p>
<h3><span id="In_King.27s_Landing"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="In_King's_Landing">In King's Landing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In King&#039;s Landing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Watching as the corpse of <a href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" class="mw-redirect" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>, the Hand of the King, is tended to, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> assures his twin sister, Queen <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a>, that if Arryn had spoken to anyone about them, they would already have been executed.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Pentos">In Pentos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In Pentos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Exiled prince <a href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" class="mw-redirect" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys Targaryen</a> plots to reclaim his father's throne from King <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>, and brokers a marriage between his sister <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a> and Dothraki warlord <a href="/wiki/Khal_Drogo" class="mw-redirect" title="Khal Drogo">Khal Drogo</a>. As wedding gifts, Daenerys is given books of the Seven Kingdoms from Ser <a href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a>, an exiled knight loyal to the Targaryens, and three petrified dragon eggs from Magister Illyrio Mopatis, who helped arrange the marriage.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_North">In the North</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In the North">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Starks of Winterfell are introduced: Lord <a href="/wiki/Ned_Stark" title="Ned Stark">Eddard "Ned" Stark</a>, his wife Lady <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a>, their children – heir <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb</a>, elder daughter <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a>, younger daughter <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a>, ten-year-old son <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran</a>, youngest son, <a href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon</a>, Ned's bastard son <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow_(character)" title="Jon Snow (character)">Jon Snow</a> and ward <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a>.
</p><p>Ned takes his sons to witness Will's execution for desertion. Ignoring Will's warning of the White Walkers, Ned beheads him, insisting Walkers are long extinct. The Starks find a dead <a href="/wiki/Stag" class="mw-redirect" title="Stag">stag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seal_(emblem)" title="Seal (emblem)">sigil</a> of House Baratheon, and a dead <a href="/wiki/Dire_wolf" title="Dire wolf">direwolf</a>, sigil of the Starks, whose pups are taken in by the children.
</p><p>News arrives of the death of Lord Arryn, Eddard's friend and Catelyn's brother-in-law. Winterfell receives the royal court, including King Robert, his wife Queen Cersei, their children – heir Prince <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>, Princess <a href="/wiki/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella</a>, and Prince <a href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a> – as well as Jaime, a member of the Kingsguard, and his and Cersei's younger brother <a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>, a dwarf known as "The Imp". Robert pays respects to <a href="/wiki/Lyanna_Stark" class="mw-redirect" title="Lyanna Stark">Lyanna Stark</a>, his late fiancée and Ned's sister, appoints Ned the new Hand of the King, and suggests Sansa be betrothed to Joffrey. Catelyn receives a message from her sister Lysa, Arryn's widow, who suspects her husband was murdered by the Lannisters. Ned reluctantly accepts the position of Hand of the King.
</p><p>Bran climbs an abandoned tower and stumbles upon Cersei and Jaime having sex. To keep the incestuous relationship a secret, Jaime shoves Bran out of the window.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conception_and_development">Conception and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conception and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A number of Hollywood studios had contacted George R.&#160;R. Martin about possibly adapting his book series <i><a href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> into a film. However, Martin expressed the opinion the books could not be made into a film as too much would have to be cut from the books, but thought it could be made into a television series.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In January 2006, David Benioff happened to speak to Martin's literary agent, and the agent sent the first four books of <i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i> to David Benioff.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Benioff read a few hundred pages of the first book in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Game_of_Thrones" title="A Game of Thrones">A Game of Thrones</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-upclose_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upclose-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> called D. B. Weiss and said: "Maybe I'm crazy, but I haven't had this much fun reading anything in about 20 years. So take a look because I think it might make a great HBO series."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Weiss, who then read the first book in two days, was very enthusiastic about a possible television project based on the books. They arranged a meeting with Martin, who asked them if they knew who Jon Snow's real mother might be, and was satisfied with their answer.<sup id="cite_ref-upclose_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upclose-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2006, a few weeks after meeting Martin, Benioff and Weiss pitched the show to <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Strauss" title="Carolyn Strauss">Carolyn Strauss</a> of HBO, the latter of whom accepted their proposal.<sup id="cite_ref-upclose_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upclose-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> HBO acquired the rights to the novels to turn them into a television series,<sup id="cite_ref-globe_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with Benioff and Weiss as writers and executive producers of the series. The series went into development in January 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-variety_1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety_1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The series would begin with the first book from 1996, "<a href="/wiki/A_Game_of_Thrones" title="A Game of Thrones">A Game of Thrones</a>", with the intention that each novel in the series would form the basis for a season's worth of episodes.<sup id="cite_ref-variety_1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety_1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, Benioff and Weiss had to resubmit a proposal after Carolyn Strauss stepped down as president of HBO in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The first and second drafts of the <a href="/wiki/Pilot_episode" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilot episode">pilot</a> script, written by Benioff and Weiss, were submitted in August 2007 and June 2008 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> While HBO found both drafts to their liking, a pilot was not ordered until November 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg/220px-D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="171" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg/330px-D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg/440px-D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg 2x" data-file-width="660" data-file-height="513" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:D._B._Weiss_and_David_Benioff.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Series co-creators <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> and <a href="/wiki/D._B._Weiss" title="D. B. Weiss">D. B. Weiss</a> wrote the episode.</div></div></div>
<p>Scripted by the show creators David Benioff and D. B. Weiss, the first episode includes the plot of <a href="/wiki/A_Game_of_Thrones" title="A Game of Thrones">the book</a>'s chapters 1–9 and 12 (Prologue, Bran I, Catelyn I, Daenerys I, Eddard I, Jon I, Catelyn II, partial Arya I, Bran II, Daenerys II). Changes in the adaptation include the sequence of events in the prologue (in the books it is Gared and not Will who survives and is beheaded by Eddard afterwards, and Arya's material is set before the arrival of the royal family), new scenes showing the Lannister twins' perspective, and Daenerys's wedding night showing Drogo not waiting for her to consent to sex.<sup id="cite_ref-westeros_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-westeros-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg/170px-Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="246" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg/255px-Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg/340px-Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="1389" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sean_Bean_TIFF_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sean Bean portrays <a href="/wiki/Ned_Stark" title="Ned Stark">Ned Stark</a> in the series.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tom_McCarthy_(director)" title="Tom McCarthy (director)">Tom McCarthy</a> was chosen to direct the pilot episode, shot between October 24 and November 19, 2009 on location in Northern Ireland, Scotland and Morocco.<sup id="cite_ref-daily_beast_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-daily_beast-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> However, the pilot was deemed unsatisfactory and had to be reshot.
</p><p>The new pilot episode was filmed in 2010 by new director <a href="/wiki/Tim_Van_Patten" title="Tim Van Patten">Tim Van Patten</a>, and several actors appearing in the original pilot did not return for the series. <a href="/wiki/Tamzin_Merchant" title="Tamzin Merchant">Tamzin Merchant</a> was replaced as Daenerys Targaryen by <a href="/wiki/Emilia_Clarke" title="Emilia Clarke">Emilia Clarke</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Ehle" title="Jennifer Ehle">Jennifer Ehle</a> was replaced as Catelyn Stark by <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fairley" title="Michelle Fairley">Michelle Fairley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mr_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mr-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Ian_McNeice" title="Ian McNeice">Ian McNeice</a> was replaced as Magister Illyrio by <a href="/wiki/Roger_Allam" title="Roger Allam">Roger Allam</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Ridings" title="Richard Ridings">Richard Ridings</a><sup id="cite_ref-3p_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3p-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> as Gared by Dermot Keaney, and <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Campbell_Bower" title="Jamie Campbell Bower">Jamie Campbell Bower</a><sup id="cite_ref-3p_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3p-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> as Ser Waymar Royce by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Ostlere" title="Rob Ostlere">Rob Ostlere</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Another difference is that the original pilot featured scenes shot in Scotland and scenes in Pentos were shot in Morocco,<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> but in the aired series, Winterfell was filmed in a combination of locations in Northern Ireland, while scenes from Pentos were from <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Doune_Castle" title="Doune Castle">Doune Castle</a> in Scotland was originally used to recreate Winterfell, and its great hall was used for some interior shots. Some scenes survived, but as it was not practical to return to Scotland for the reshoot, an exact replica of Doune's great hall was recreated in the soundstage in Belfast for the series. <a href="/wiki/Castle_Ward" title="Castle Ward">Castle Ward</a> in Northern Ireland was used in the reshoot to film King Robert's entourage entry into Winterfell castle.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> A car park stood in for the Winterfell castle's courtyard and a wine cellar for the Stark family crypt.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tollymore_Forest_Park" title="Tollymore Forest Park">Tollymore Forest Park</a> was used for the opening scene of the encounter with the White Walkers.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All the scenes shot in Morocco were reshot in Malta.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The original pilot reused the sets of <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Heaven_(film)" title="Kingdom of Heaven (film)">Kingdom of Heaven</a></i> in Morocco to stand in for Pentos and the site of Drogo and Daenerys's wedding.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In Malta, the <a href="/wiki/Verdala_Palace" title="Verdala Palace">Verdala Palace</a>, the 16th century summer palace of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Malta" title="President of Malta">president of Malta</a>, was used for the exterior scenes at Illyrio's mansion. The <a href="/wiki/Azure_Window" title="Azure Window">Azure Window</a> was used as the backdrop for the wedding.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Filming at the Azure Window, however, caused some controversy when a protected ecosystem was damaged by a subcontractor.<sup id="cite_ref-ecosystem_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ecosystem-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the sex scene, the then-pregnant <a href="/wiki/Lena_Headey" title="Lena Headey">Lena Headey</a> was substituted by a <a href="/wiki/Body_double" title="Body double">body double</a>; the production hid her pregnancy for the rest of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In the scene in which the Starks encounter a stag killed by a dire wolf as they return from the execution, an actual animal was used rather than a prop. As the stag had been dead for two days, it stank so much that the actors had to take much care not to let it show on their faces.<sup id="cite_ref-Den_of_Geek_29_February_2012_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Den_of_Geek_29_February_2012-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Some scenes filmed were never aired, for example a flashback to the death of Eddard Stark's brother, and the death of Jon Arryn.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_pilot">Original pilot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Original pilot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original pilot from 2009 was poorly received in a private viewing with friends, one of whom, <a href="/wiki/Craig_Mazin" title="Craig Mazin">Craig Mazin</a>, said to Benioff and Weiss, "You guys have a massive problem", and said "change everything" when asked for ideas.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> It was so disliked that Kit Harington joked that when he annoys Benioff and Weiss, they threaten to release the episode on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nicholson20171021_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nicholson20171021-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Weiss said of the viewing: "Watching them watch the pilot was a deeply humiliating, painful experience, because these are very smart individuals, and it just clearly wasn’t working for any of them on a very basic level."<sup id="cite_ref-vanity_fair_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vanity_fair-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> For example, it was never established that Jaime and Cersei Lannister were in fact brother and sister, a major plot point.<sup id="cite_ref-variety_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>HBO did not make a decision for four months after the pilot was delivered.<sup id="cite_ref-variety_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In March 2010, HBO's decision to greenlight the series including the pilot was announced,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> with the production of the series scheduled to start June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> HBO however demanded extensive reshoot of the pilot, and wanted all the scenes from Morocco scrapped.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A cameo appearance by George RR Martin as a Pentoshi nobleman at Daenerys's wedding filmed in Morocco was therefore also cut.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In all 90 percent of the pilot was re-shot in 2010, with some cast changes and a different director.<sup id="cite_ref-vanity_fair_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vanity_fair-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aired_episode">Aired episode</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aired episode">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The original pilot remained unaired, although some footage from the original pilot was used in the first aired episode.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> This includes Sansa's scenes with Catelyn (Michelle Fairley's footage as Catelyn was inserted over Jennifer Ehle's performance),<sup id="cite_ref-vanity_fair_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vanity_fair-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Will's ride through the woods (retained though also portrayed by a different actor), most of the feast at Winterfell, and Ned and Robert's scene in the crypt. That scene is one of a few to be filmed on <a href="/wiki/35mm_movie_film" class="mw-redirect" title="35mm movie film">35 mm film</a>, and consequently slight <a href="/wiki/Film_grain" title="Film grain">film grain</a> can be seen in the HD version of the episode.<sup id="cite_ref-Den_of_Geek_29_February_2012_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Den_of_Geek_29_February_2012-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Preview">Preview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Preview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 3, 2011, two weeks before the series premiere aired, the first 15 minutes of "Winter Is Coming" were released as a preview on HBO's website.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Wired_(magazine)" title="Wired (magazine)">Wired</a>'</i>s Dave Banks called the preview "much better than anticipated."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Scott Stinson of Toronto's <a href="/wiki/National_Post" title="National Post">National Post</a> noted that "you know you aren’t watching a network drama when there have been two beheadings in the first 15 minutes."<sup id="cite_ref-np_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-np-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first episode of <i>Game of Thrones</i> obtained 2.2 million viewers in its premiere airing, with an additional 2 million viewers in the reruns aired during the same night. The day after the premiere HBO aired the episode six additional times, adding another 1.2 million to the viewer's figures.<sup id="cite_ref-insidetv1_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insidetv1-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Reruns aired during the following week upped the total viewership to 6.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-insidetv2_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insidetv2-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The show premiered on <a href="/wiki/HBO_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="HBO Canada">HBO Canada</a> at the same time as its U.S. premiere.<sup id="cite_ref-np_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-np-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> On April 18, 2011, the show premiered in United Kingdom and Ireland through <a href="/wiki/Sky_Atlantic" title="Sky Atlantic">Sky Atlantic</a>, gathering 750,000 viewers, a ratings record for the network.<sup id="cite_ref-insidetv1_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insidetv1-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The series was broadcast throughout Latin America beginning on May 8, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> New Zealand's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dominion_Post_(Wellington)" title="The Dominion Post (Wellington)">Dominion Post</a></i> noted in an article on copyright laws that the popular series was downloaded via file sharing service regularly before its release to that market.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In Australia, the July 17 premiere of the series was largely overshadowed by the release of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Dance_with_Dragons" title="A Dance with Dragons">A Dance with Dragons</a></i>, but according to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sydney_Morning_Herald" title="The Sydney Morning Herald">The Sydney Morning Herald</a></i> was successful "especially with women, who aren't seen as a target market for sword-fighting sagas".<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_Is_Coming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The critical response to the first episode of the series was positive. <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">Review aggregator</a> <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> surveyed 15 reviews of the episode and judged 100% of them to be positive with an average score of 8.5 out of 10. The website's critical consensus reads, <span style="padding-right:.15em;">"</span>'Winter is Coming' is an introduction to a wonderfully bleak journey that honors its source material with stellar execution and an impressive cast."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> James Poniewozik from <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> considered it an "epic win",<sup id="cite_ref-time_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> and Jace Lacob from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Beast" title="The Daily Beast">The Daily Beast</a></i> deemed it "unforgettable".<sup id="cite_ref-Televisionary_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Televisionary-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/HitFix" title="HitFix">HitFix</a>'s Alan Sepinwall wrote that while it was too early to say if <i>Game of Thrones</i> belonged to the HBO pantheon with shows like <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a></i>, it had many things in common with those shows.<sup id="cite_ref-hitfix_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hitfix-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'s Matt Fowler wrote that the pilot "effortlessly takes us along, faithfully, through the book, but it also manages to capture the majestically morbid spirit of Martin's pages and turn them into thrilling television".<sup id="cite_ref-IGN_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Much praise was given to the production values and the acting: Scott Meslow from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Atlantic" title="The Atlantic">The Atlantic</a></i> states that "the show's immense cast is almost universally strong, and the fantasy land of Westeros feels lived-in, and looks terrific".<sup id="cite_ref-atlantic_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlantic-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Alan Sepinwall also qualifies the casting as "really exceptional", and states that the show is "feast for the eyes", with all the different locations having their own memorable looks.<sup id="cite_ref-hitfix_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hitfix-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The opening sequence, with an aerial view of the world where the series takes place with the different settings emerging from it, was also acclaimed.<sup id="cite_ref-hitfix_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hitfix-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlantic_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlantic-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 19, less than two days after the initial airing, HBO announced that the series had been renewed for a second season.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> In a press teleconference, HBO executives announced their satisfaction with initial ratings, which they compared favorably to <i><a href="/wiki/True_Blood" title="True Blood">True Blood</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
